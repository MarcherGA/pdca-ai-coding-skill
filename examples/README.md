## üéØ Example Use Cases

### Full PDCA (Complex Tasks)
- **New feature with cross-system integration**
  - Example: Payment gateway integration
  - Duration: 2-3 hours
  - All 5 phases fully utilized

- **Architectural changes**
  - Example: Refactoring auth system
  - Duration: 2-3 hours
  - Heavy analysis and planning

- **Novel problem domains**
  - Example: Implementing WebSocket support
  - Duration: 2-3 hours
  - Multiple alternative approaches considered

### Lightweight PDCA (Simple Tasks)
- **Implementing an interface with clear examples**
  - Example: Adding new REST endpoint following existing pattern
  - Duration: 30-60 minutes
  - Skip detailed analysis, focus on TDD

- **Extending existing functionality**
  - Example: Adding field validation to existing form
  - Duration: 30-45 minutes
  - Use patterns from analysis, quick planning

### Emergency PDCA (Bug Fixes)
- **Production bug requiring immediate fix**
  - Example: Users can't log in
  - Duration: 15-30 minutes
  - Minimal analysis, reproduce bug test, fix, verify

## üìù Want to Contribute Your Examples?

We'd love to see your real-world PDCA sessions! 

**To contribute:**
1. Anonymize your session log (remove proprietary details)
2. Use the [session-template.md](../assets/session-template.md)
3. Include retrospective and metrics
4. Submit PR with your example

**What makes a good example:**
- Shows realistic challenges and solutions
- Documents human interventions
- Includes retrospective learnings
- Shows metrics and quality tracking
- Demonstrates different complexity levels

## üéì Learning Path

**Week 1-2: Follow the Example**
- Read [session-log-example.md](session-log-example.md)
- Try similar feature in your codebase
- Compare your session to the example
- Note differences and learnings

**Week 3-4: Document Your Own**
- Use session-template.md for new features
- Focus on capturing interventions
- Complete thorough retrospectives
- Track your metrics

**Month 2: Optimize Your Process**
- Review all your session logs
- Identify common patterns
- Refine your prompts
- Share what you learned
